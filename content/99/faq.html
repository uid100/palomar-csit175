Either is fine. I recommend using an IDE if you are new to programming. An IDE can help you manage your code files, provide syntax highlighting, and offer debugging tools that can make the coding process easier.

 

If you are comfortable with a text editor and prefer its simplicity, that's perfectly acceptable as well. The key is to choose a tool that you feel comfortable with and that supports your learning style.

Some popular IDEs for Python include PyCharm, Visual Studio Code, and Jupyter Notebook. For text editors, options like Sublime Text, Atom, or even Notepad++ can work well.

Ultimately, the choice between an IDE and a text editor comes down to personal preference and what helps you be most productive in your coding journey.

I do not grade on attendance.

Class meetings are scheduled to help students better understand the curriculum and to help the instructor more effectively communicate ideas to students.

I believe class participation is beneficial. I think that through our conversations, I can better assess how material is being received and understood. With feedback we can speed up and slow down through different topics within schedule boundaries.

The HyFlex classroom is designed to support those students, but there is still an expectation that the student is responsible for hosting a suitable remote classroom environment, with adequate technical resources and as free from distractions as possible.
