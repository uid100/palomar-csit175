Still in chapter 3, we continue our discussion of coding control statements. In the last module, we looked at selection structures (if-then-else). Selection structures allow us to make decisions in our code based on conditions that evaluate to true or false.

Let's look at the third (and last) fundamental coding structure: Loops or repetition structures. In this chapter, we'll see how we can repeat an instruction (or block of instructions) as long as some condition holds true. Just like selection structures, loop conditions must evaluate to a true/false result.
